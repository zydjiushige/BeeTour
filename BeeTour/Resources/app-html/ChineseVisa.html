Chinese Visa

1. Basic concepts

Visas are issued by authorized government authorities of a State in accordance with national laws and regulations. A visa permits the foreign to enter, exit from or transit through the host country.

In accordance with international law and international practice, any sovereign country has the right to decide whether to allow foreigners to enter or exit the country, and in accordance with national law any sovereign country can determine whether to issue a visa, decline a visa application or cancel an issued visa.

The Chinese consular officials in accordance with the Chinese laws and regulations determine the type, number of entries, validity and period of each stay of a visa to be issued, they also can decline a visa application or revoke an issued visa.



2. Visa-issuing authorities

Chinese embassies, consulates and other institutions stationed abroad entrusted by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of China are visa-issuing authorities responsible for issuance of entry visas to foreigner citizens abroad.

Inside the country the Ministry of Foreign Affairs is the authority responsible for the foreign affairs, and the Ministry of Public Security is the authority responsible for the public security.



3. Types of visa and required documents

Chinese visas fall into four types: diplomatic visa, courtesy visa, service visa and ordinary visa.

Ordinary visas are further divided into 8 sub-types:

1. Tourism visa (L Visa): Foreigners who intend to come to China for travelling, visiting relatives or other personal reasons for short-term.

2. Visit visa (F Visa): Foreigners who are invited to go to China for technological and cultural exchanges, visit, study tours and other non-business activities for no more than 6 months.

3. Student visa (X Visa): Foreigners who intend to study in China for more than 6 months.

4. Work visa (Z Visa): Foreigners who intend to work in China. It is also issued to the foreign workers' accompanying spouses and under age children.

5. Transit visa (G Visa): Foreigners who intend to transit through China and have to remain for more than 24 hours in Chinese airports.

6. Crew visa (C Visa):Foreign crew members of means of international transportation, including aircraft, trains and ships, or motor vehicle drivers engaged in cross-border transport activities.

7. Journalist visa (J Visa): This visa falls into 2 types, J1 and J2.

J1 Visa: Resident foreign journalists. It is also issued to the foreign journalists' accompanying spouses and under age children.

J2 Visa: Foreign journalists who intend to go to China for short-term.

8. Permanent visa (D Visa): Foreigners who intend to reside in China permanently.

The Chinese Visa authorities work in accordance to the law, and decide which type of visa will be issued according to the applicant identity, purpose of coming and type of passport.

5. Visa validity

Visa Validity ("Enter Before") means that the visa is valid, or can be used for entry into China from the date of issue to the "Enter Before" date indicated on the visa (Beijing Time). If a visa has unused entries, the bearer can enter China one day before (Beijing Time) on the expiration date.



6. Visa entries

"Entries" refers to the number of times the foreign is permitted to enter in China during the validity of a visa. A visa becomes invalid if there are no entries left, or there are entries left but the visa validity expires.

If a visa becomes invalid, the foreign must apply for a new visa before entering China. Traveling with an invalid visa to China will result in refusal of entry into the country.



7. Duration

The visa duration is related to the "Duration of Each Stay". It refers to the maximum number of days the visa bearer is permitted to stay in China each time, which is calculated from the date of entry into China.

If the foreign exceeds the period allowed by the visa to stay in China and have not handle the formalities to postpone its visa validity, the foreign will be violating the People`s Republic of China administration of entry and exit of foreigners law and regulations, and so shall be punished with fines and other penalties.

Therefore, the holder of a Chinese visa who plans to remain in China beyond the allowed duration of stay needs to ask for an extension of his visa validity to the local public security organs before its visa validity expiries (for specific requirements please consult your local police department website). The extension request will be analyzed before approval or refusal.

The Chinese embassies and consulates are not responsible for the Chinese visa extension.

For those who work in China (Z Visa), study (X Visa), hold a permanent visa (D Visa), or are resident foreign journalists (J-1 Visa) it is necessary to go to the local public security authorities within 30 days of entry into China for managing the Chinese legal residence permit formalities.

Members of foreign diplomatic or consular missions in China must apply for a residence permit at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or local foreign affairs departments within 30 days of entry into China.



8. Necessary supporting documents

Basic documents

1.Passport: Passport with at least 6 months of remaining validity and blank visa pages. It is also necessary 1 copy of the passport's data and photo page.

2.Visa application form and photo:One completed Visa Application Form with a passport photo attached.The photo must be recently-taken, colored, bare-headed, full-faced and with a light background.

If a visa becomes invalid, the foreign must apply for a new visa before entering China. Traveling with an invalid visa to China will result in refusal of entry into the country.

3.Legal stay or residence certificate (applicable to those who are not in their country when applying for the visa): If you are not in your country when applying for the Chinese visa, you must provide the original and the copy of your legal stay, residence, employment or study certificate.

4.Previous Chinese passport or previous Chinese visa (Applicable to foreign citizens who were Chinese citizens and have obtained foreign citizenship): If you are applying for the Chinese visa for the first time, you should provide the previous Chinese passport held and a photocopy of its data page. If you have obtained Chinese visas before and want to apply for a Chinese visa with a renewed foreign passport that does not contain any Chinese visa, you should present the photocopy of the previous passport's data page and the photo page if it is separate, as well as the previous Chinese visa page. (If your name on the current passport differs from that on the previous one, you must provide an official document of name change.)
