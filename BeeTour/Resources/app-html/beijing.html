Beijing

Beijing is the capital of the People's Republic of China and one of the most populous cities in the world, with the 3rd most populous in the world, with a total population of 21,150,000.

Geography

Beijing is located in the northern tip of the roughly triangular North China Plain, which opens to the south and east of the city. The urban area of Beijing, on the plains in the south-central of the municipality with elevation of 40 to 60 meters (130–200 feet), occupies a relatively small but expanding portion of the municipality's area.

Climate

The monthly daily average temperature in January is −3.7 °C (25.3 °F), while in July it is 26.2 °C (79.2 °F). Precipitation averages around 570 mm (22 in) annually, with close to three-fourths of that total falling from June to August. With monthly percent possible sunshine ranging from 47% in July to 65% in January and February, the city receives 2,671 hours of bright sunshine annually.

Transportation

Rail and high-speed rail

Beijing is a large rail hub in China's railway network, and has three high-speed rail lines: the Beijing-Tianjin Intercity Railway, the Beijing-Shanghai High-Speed Railway, and the Beijing–Guangzhou High-Speed Railway.

Roads and expressways

Beijing is connected by road links to all parts of China as part of the National Trunk Road Network. Nine expressways of China serve Beijing, as do eleven China National Highways. Beijing's urban transport is dependent upon the five "ring roads" that concentrically surround the city, with the Forbidden City area marked as the geographical center for the ring roads.

Air

Beijing Capital International Airport is the primary airport in Beijing ,which is around 20 kilometers northeast of the city center and is the main hub for Air China and a hub for China Southern and Hainan Airlines. The airport links Beijing with almost every other Chinese city with regular air passenger service. The Airport Expressway links the airport to central Beijing; it is a roughly 40-minute drive from the city center during good traffic conditions. On the other hand,Liangxiang,Nanyuan,Xijiao,Shahe andBadaling airport are primarily for military use and are less well known to the public.

Public transit

On December 28, 2014, the Beijing Subway switched to a distance-based fare system from a fixed fare for all lines except the Airport Express. Under the new system a trip under 6 km will cost ¥3.00, an additional ¥1.00 will be added for the next 6 kilometers (3.7 miles) and the next 10 kilometers (6.2 miles) until the distance for the trip reaches 32 kilometers (20 miles).[156] For every 20 kilometers (12 miles) after the original 32 kilometers (20 miles) an additional ¥1.00 is added.

Taxi

Metered taxi in Beijing start at ¥13,which include the first 3kilometers,¥2.3 per additional 1 kilometer and ¥1 per ride fuel surcharge, not counting idling fees which are ¥2.3 (¥4.6 during rush hours of 7-9 am and 5-7pm) per 5 minutes of standing or running at speeds lower than 12 kilometers per hour. After 15 kilometers, the base fare increases by 50%. Between 11 pm and 5 am, 20% fee is increased. Rides over 15 km and between 23:00 and 06:00 incur both charges, for a total increase of 80%. Tolls during trip should be covered by customers and the costs of trips beyond Beijing city limits should be negotiated with the driver. The cost of unregistered taxis is also subject to negotiation with the driver.

Bicycles

Bicycles play an important role in the local transportation. Large numbers of cyclists can be seen on most roads in the city, and most of the main roads have dedicated bicycle lanes.

Economy

Beijing's economy ranks among the most developed and prosperous in China. In 2013, the municipality's nominal gross domestic product (GDP) was CN￥1.95 trillion (US$314 billion), about 3.43% of the country's total output, and ranked 13th among province-level administrative units. Per capita GDP, at CN￥93,213 (US$15,051) in nominal terms and Int$21,948 at purchasing power parity, was 2.2 times the national average and ranked second among province-level administrative units. The economy tripled in size from 2004 to 2012, and grew at an annual rate of 7.7% in 2013.

Demographics

Beijing is one of the most populous cities in the world, with a total population of 21,150,000.

Culture

Beijing or Peking opera (京剧, Jīngjù) is a traditional form of Chinese theater well known throughout the nation, which is named as one of the highest achievements of Chinese culture, Beijing opera is performed through a combination of song, spoken dialogue, and codified action sequences involving gestures, movement, fighting and acrobatics. Much of Beijing opera is carried out in an archaic stage dialect quite different from Modern Standard Chinese and from the modern Beijing dialect.

Peking Roast Duck is perhaps the best famous dish in Beijing .Fuling and Jianbing are also a traditional Beijing snack food.

Places of interest

At the historical heart of Beijing lies the Forbidden City where hosts the Palace Museum, which contains imperial collections of Chinese art. From Tiananmen Square, right across from the Forbidden City, there are several notable sites, such as the Tiananmen, Qianmen, the Great Hall of the People, the National Museum of China, the Monument to the People's Heroes, and the Mausoleum of Mao Zedong. Beihai Park is also a famous sight,which are described as masterpieces of Chinese gardening art, and popular tourist destinations with tremendous historical importance; in the modern era, Zhongnanhai has also been the political heart of various Chinese governments and regimes and is now the headquarters of the Communist Party of China and the State Council.The Summer Palace and the Old Summer Palace both lie at the western part of the city,contains a comprehensive collection of imperial gardens and palaces that served as the summer retreats for the Qing imperial family.
