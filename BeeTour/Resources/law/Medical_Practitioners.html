• Law of the people’s Republic of China on Medical Practitioners

    • General Provisions

    This Law is enacted for the purpose of strengthening a contingent of doctors, improving occupational morals and professional skills of doctors, safeguarding legitimate rights and interests of doctors, and protecting the people’s health.

    This Law is applicable to professional medical workers who are qualified as medical practitioners or assistant medical practitioners according to law and registered to practice medicine in institutions of medical treatment, prevention or health care. "Doctors" referred to in this Law include medical practitioners and assistant medical practitioners.

    The whole society should respect doctors. Doctors are protected by law in performing duties according to law.

    The administrative department of health under the State Council shall be responsible for the work for doctors throughout the country. Administrative departments of health under local people’s governments at or above the county level shall be responsible for the work for doctors within their respective administrative regions.

    The state rewards doctors who have made contributions to medical treatment, prevention of diseases and health care.

    Medical professional and technical titles of doctors and the appraisal and appointment of medical professional and technical posts for doctors shall be handled according to relevant state provisions.

    Doctors may organize and join the doctors’ association according to law.

    Examination and Registration

    The state practices an examination system for the qualifications of doctors.
