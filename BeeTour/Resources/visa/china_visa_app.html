• L Visa

    • What’s the definition of China L Visa?

    L Visa (Tourist) is issued to those who intend to go to China as a tourist.

    Basic Documents for L Visa application

    Required Documents are as follows:

    1. The applicant' passport with at least 6 months remaining validity and available blank pages.
    2. One truly completed Visa Application Form.
    3. A recent passport-size color photo with white background stuck on the application form.
    4. Proof of legal stay or residence status (applicable to those not applying in their country of citizenship)
    5. Documents showing the itinerary including round trip air ticket booking record and proof of hotel reservation, etc. Or Invitation letter issued by a relevant entity or individual in China, with following information:
      1. Information on the applicant (full name, gender, date of birth, etc.)
      2. Information on the planned visit (arrival and departure dates, place(s) of visit, etc.)
      3. Information on the inviting entity or individual (name, contact number, address, official stamp, signature of the legal representative of the entity or the inviting individual)

    The Procedure for China L Visa application

    1. You shall come to the visa office of the Embassy or Consulate-general in the consular jurisdiction where you reside to submit the application;
    2. If you cannot come personally, you may send someone else or a travel/visa agent to come to the visa office of the Embassy or Consulate-General in the consular jurisdiction where you reside for application process;
    3. Mail applications not acceptable and will be returned;
    4. No appointment is required.

    Processing time

    1. The regular processing time is 4 working days.
    2. Express service: 2-3 working days processing;
    3. Same day rush service

  • M Visa

    • What’s the definition of China M Visa?

    M Visa(Commercial Trade) is issued to those who intend to go to China for commercial and trade activities.

    Basic Documents for China M Visa application

    Required documents are as follows:

    1. Application Form with a recently-taken color photo glued to;
    2. Valid passport (with at least 6months Validity) and a copy of information page;
    3. One of the following documents:
      1. Invitation letter issued by the Chinese business or trade partner;
      2. Invitation letter or confirmation letter of Invitation issued by duly authorized Chinese institutes or relevant units;
      3. Invitation letter of Trade Fair;
      4. Business License or Certificate approving the establishment of enterprise which shows the applicant as the enterprise's investor or owner;
    4. For application of a multiple entry visa: copy of all previous Chinese visas (on one A-4 sized paper) should be attached;
    5. Personal written statement (covering letter with details as follows: place to visit, name, address and contact number of the inviting / contact company or person);
    6. Other documents required by the Chinese Embassy.

    Where can foreigners apply for China M Visa?

    1. You shall come to the visa office of Chinese Embassy or Consulate-general in the consular jurisdiction where you reside to submit the application;
    2. If you cannot come personally, you may send someone else or a travel/visa agent to come to the visa office of the Embassy or Consulate-General in the consular jurisdiction where you reside for application process;
    3. Mail applications not acceptable and will be returned;
    4. No appointment is required

    The Procedure for China M Visa

    1. Step1, the enterprise should provide relevant materials;
    2. Step2, the enterprise staff with the relevant materials (all materials must be in duplicate),comes to Foreign Affairs Division of local Commerce Bureau for preliminary examination;
    3. Step3 After the approval of preliminary examination, then get the official seal signed on Foreign Affairs Office visa application notification form by local Foreign Affairs office of Commerce Bureau;
    4. Step4, the enterprise staff takes the required documents with signature and seal to Local Foreign Affairs Office for final review;
    5. When the applicant get his or her required documents provided by the enterprise, then the applicant comes to the visa office of Chinese Embassy or Consulate-general in the consular jurisdiction where you reside to submit the application;
    6. After several working days ,the applicant can obtain his/her China M Visa.

    Processing time

    1. The regular processing time is 4 working days.
    2. Express service: 2-3 working days processing;
    3. Same day rush service

  • Z Visa (Work)

    • What’s the definition of China Z Visa?

    Z Visa is issued to those who intend to work in China.

    Basic Documents for China Z Visa application

    1. Applicant's valid actual passport with at least six months validity left before expiration and at least one blank page left in it;
    2. One truly completed Visa Application Form;
    3. One recent passport standard photo affixed on the Application Form;
    4. Invitation Letter issued by duly authorized entity or Confirmation Letter of Invitation issued by relevant Chinese entities;
    5. Submit Work Permit (one of the following work permits):
      1. Foreigners Employment Permit issued by the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security of the PRC;
      2. Permit for Foreign Experts (Foreign Experts Certificate) issued by the State Bureau of Foreign Experts of the PRC;
      3. An approval document for commercial performances issued by the Chinese government authorities for cultural affairs;
      4. Invitation Letter to Foreigners for Offshore Petroleum Operations issued by National Offshore Oil Corporation;

    Where can foreigners apply for China Z Visa (Work Visa)?

    1. You shall come to the visa office of the Embassy or Consulate-general in the consular jurisdiction where you reside to submit the application;
    2. If you cannot come personally, you may send someone else or a travel/visa agent to come to the visa office of the Embassy or Consulate-General in the consular jurisdiction where you reside for application process;
    3. Mail applications not acceptable and will be returned;
    4. No appointment is required.

    The Procedure for China Z Visa

    1. Step one, Chinese employing units go to Human Resources and Social Security Bureau to apply for Foreigner Employment Permit;
    2. Step two, take the Employment Permit and the Visa Notification Application Form to the local Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation Bureau to handle employment visa invitation letter.
    3. Step three, the applicant should bring the Employment Permit, the invitation letter and his/her passport to Chinese Embassy or Consulate-General for handling China Z Visa.
    4. Step four, within 15 days after the entry , the foreigner applicant should go to Human Resources and Social Security Department to get the Foreigner Work/ Employment Certificate.
    5. Within thirty (30) days after entry, the applicant should bring Employment certificate, passport and other relevant materials to the Exit and Entry Administration of local Public Security Bureau to deal with Residence Permit.

    Processing time

    1. The regular processing time is 4 working days.
    2. Express service: 2-3 working days processing;
    3. Same day rush service

    4. Copy of the inviter’s China ID card or copy of the inviter’s foreigner Passport and Permanent Residence Permit;

    C Visa brief introduction
    C Visa (Crew Visa) is issued to a crew member performing his/her duties on board of an international train, airliner, ocean-liner or freighter.
    Please provide the following documents:

    1. Application Form with a recently-taken color photo glued to.
    2. Valid passport and a copy of information page.
    3. The covering letter and guarantee letter issued by the foreign airlines or Shipping company, or the invitation letter issued by the relevant units from China.
    4. Other documents required by the Chinese Embassy.
    5. If you’re sailor applicants, you should provide original and copies of SD, the CDC, SID

    J Visa

    J Visa Brief Introduction
    J Visa (Journalist Visa) is issued to foreign journalists. J-visa has two categories: J-I visa and J-2 visa. J-1 visa is issued to resident foreign journalists in China and their accompanying spouses and under-age children; J-2 visa is issued to foreign journalists who visit China for temporary news coverage.

    G Visa

    G Visa Brief Introduction
    G Visa (Transit visas) are for persons who are staying for a short period while in transit between two other countries.
    Requirements are as follows:

    1. Applicant's valid passport must have at least six months of remaining validity with at least one blank visa page in it.
    2. One truly completed Application Form.
    3. One color passport standard photo (2X2 square inch) glued or stapled on the application form.
    4. A valid visa to the country of destination if required.
    5. An air/train/ship ticket to the final destination with confirmed departure date and seats.

    D Visa brief introduction
    D Visa (Permanent Residence Visa) is issued to a foreign citizen who has been approved by China’s public security authority to reside in China permanently. Basic Documents are as follows:

    1. Application Form with a recently-taken color photo glued to.
    2. Valid passport and a copy of information page.
    3. Original copy of the Certificate of Confirmation for foreigner's Permanent Residence Status issued by the Ministry of Public Security of the People's Republic of China.
    4. Personal written statement (covering letter with details as follows: place of destination in China, name, address and contact number of the inviting / contact company / person).
    5. Other documents required by the Chinese Embassy.
